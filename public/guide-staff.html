Staff Guide

Running the day-to-day

The admin portal is where staff price and confirm bids, manage bookings, instructors, members, adventures, properties, and waivers. This guide covers the work every staff role shares. A handful of controls are Admin only — see the admin guide.

Key terms

Bid
A guest’s booking-in-progress that you price, fill with content, and confirm or deny. The spine of the workflow.
Quote vs deposit
The quote is the full price the guest sees; the deposit is the minimum due before the trip.
Horizon
How far ahead a property accepts bookings (a per-property setting).
Catalog
A property’s services (disciplines) and add-ons shown in the public funnel.
Template
A reusable FAQ/gear item (or waiver wording) that auto-fills onto bids.

2. Who can sign in & your role

Five staff roles share the admin portal. You all see the same screens; what differs is what your writes can touch (the system narrows your data and blocks out-of-scope actions behind the scenes).

RoleWhat it does day-to-day
super_admin / adminEverything in this guide, plus the Admin only controls (team, creating instructors, waiver templates).
property_managerThe full day-to-day, scoped to their own property — they see and manage their property’s bids, bookings, instructors, adventures, and waivers.
conciergeDay-to-day bid, booking, and guest work.
membership_coordinatorDay-to-day work with a membership focus.

If you try something outside your scope, you’ll get a clear “You don’t have permission…” message rather than a broken page. That’s the system protecting data, not a bug. Admins intentionally do not sign in to the member or instructor portals — you see that data through admin screens.

A few features are reserved for admins. They’re marked Admin only throughout this guide and covered fully in the admin guide: the Team roster, creating/deleting instructors, and waiver templates.

3. First sign-in & your profile

  1. Accept your invite. An admin invites you by email. Click the link to sign in. (You can set a password later to skip the email next time.)
  2. Set your name on the Welcome screen. The first time in, you’re asked to enter your Full name and tap “Continue to the portal.” You can’t use the portal until you do — it’s a one-time step.
  3. Tweak your profile anytime. Click your email/role at the top-right to open Your profile: edit your name, and optionally set a password (“you can always use the link as a backup”).

The top nav holds everything: Dashboard, Bids (with a badge counting bids awaiting review), Bookings, Instructors, Adventures, Members, Properties, FAQ & Gear, Waivers, Team Admin only, and What’s New. Sign out from the top-right.

4. The dashboard

The landing screen is a read-only operations snapshot. Four cards:

  • Pending review — a count plus the most recent waiting bids. “Open queue →” jumps to the bids list filtered to pending.
  • Recent activity — the latest bids by update time, color-coded by status.
  • Confirmed · next 24 hours — a per-property schedule for today and tomorrow.
  • Upcoming · next 7 days — one column per property.

Every row links into a detail page. All times are Central Time (CT).

5. Working a bid — the core job

A bid is a guest’s booking-in-progress. Staff price it, fill in its content, and either Confirm it (which unlocks signing & paying for the guest) or Deny it.

The queue

From Bids, filter with the workflow pills — All · Needs review · Active · Closed — and refine Active by Awaiting guest / Signed / Paid. There’s also search (guest name/email), a property dropdown, and a date range. The table shows guest, booking, when, property, status, and created time; whole rows are clickable.

Inside a bid

Open a bid to see the full picture: the booking, guest contact, disciplines & add-ons, the pricing breakdown, lifecycle timestamps, the waiver roster, and internal staff notes. Two editors and a set of actions live here.

Edit the content (the slide-over drawer)

Edit bid content opens a drawer where you set what the guest sees: Schedule notes, a Gear list, and FAQ entries (all support markdown), plus internal Staff notes. You can pull from the library (the FAQ & Gear templates) to auto-fill, or “Re-pull from library” to refresh.

Set the price

In the Pricing editor, set the Confirmed quote (what the guest pays — overrides the auto-estimate), the Deposit (minimum due before the trip), and a Quote note explaining any change. If add-ons changed since the last quote, you’ll see the delta and a one-click “Apply suggested quote.”

Act on the bid

ActionWhenWhat it does
ConfirmBid is in reviewUnlocks the schedule, waiver, and deposit on the guest’s page; emails the guest.
DenyBid is in reviewMarks it denied with an internal reason (the guest sees only “closed,” not the reason).
Copy / Rotate URLActive bidsCopy the guest’s secure link, or rotate it to issue a new one and invalidate the old (use only if a link leaked).
Refund depositDeposit was paidRefunds via Stripe (full or partial), which moves the bid to refunded and cancels the booking (the slot frees up).

Always verify the price and content before confirming — confirming is what reveals it all to the guest.

Waivers on a bid

For a confirmed or paid bid, the page shows a “X of N signed” roster and lets you collect waivers three ways: a Scan-to-sign QR (each guest signs on their own phone), Sign in person (open it on an iPad and hand it over), and a link to view any signed PDF.

Bid status, end to end

Every bid moves through a fixed set of statuses. Here’s what each means — and the friendlier label the guest sees on their own page. (The queue’s Needs review / Active / Closed pills group these: Active = confirmed, signed, or paid; Closed = denied, expired, or refunded.)

Status (what you see)MeaningGuest sees
Pending reviewSubmitted by a guest; waiting for you to price and confirm.“In review”
ConfirmedYou approved and priced it; the guest can now sign and pay.“Confirmed”
SignedWaiver signed; a deposit may still be owed.“Signed”
Paid / BookedDeposit paid and waiver signed — fully finalized.“Booked”
DeniedYou couldn’t accommodate it; your reason stays internal.“Closed”
ExpiredSat unsigned/unpaid past its window.“Expired”
RefundedA paid deposit was returned; the booking is cancelled.“Refunded”

6. Bookings

A calendar-first view of all bookings, with a property filter and a two-month calendar colored by how busy each day is. Pick a day to see its per-property schedule. There’s a “Book for a customer” button that walks you through the normal booking funnel on a customer’s behalf (the booking is attributed to you).

Most bookings have a bid — opening one takes you straight to its bid detail, which is where the work happens. The standalone booking page is read-only and rarely needed.

7. Instructors

The Instructors area manages the roster and the read-only “Gameplan” portal instructors sign into. Any staff role can view it; managing requires being an admin or the property manager for that instructor’s property.

  • Edit a profile — photo, bio, the properties they teach at, the disciplines they’re qualified for, an Active toggle, and display order. (Photo + bio appear in the guest’s lesson picker.)
  • Schedule — “Weekly schedule & time off”: set per-property weekly hours and date exceptions (the same editor instructors use themselves).
  • Portal access — Send invite to a new instructor, Resend link if theirs was lost, or Revoke to cut off access (keeps their record and history).

Admin only Creating a brand-new instructor and deleting one are reserved for admins. To retire someone with bookings, toggle them inactive rather than deleting — history is preserved. See the admin guide.

8. Members directory

A read-only directory of memberships — you don’t sign in as a member. Filter by status, search by name/email/member number, and filter by property or tier. Open a person to see their memberships (per property, with member #, status, role, household size, join date), their bookings, and their adventure RSVPs.

9. Adventures

Curated, members-only trips. The index lists each adventure with its property, dates, status, payment mode, and how many spots are booked. New adventure opens a rich editor:

  • Basics — property, title, description, dates, capacity, max guests per reservation.
  • Pricing & payment — price, per-guest fee, deposit, a payment mode (Instant / Deposit / Inquire), and the free-cancellation window.
  • Visibility — Draft vs Published, and “Mark as sold out.”
  • Display — card & hero imagery (auto-optimized), category, location, highlights, and chapters.

On a saved adventure, the roster lists reservations. You can Confirm an inquiry lead, Refund & cancel (full refund), or Cancel (no refund), and Export CSV of the party list. An adventure with reservations can’t be hard-deleted — set it to draft/cancelled instead.

10. Properties & catalog

Property Settings has one editable card per property:

  • Booking rules — the horizon (how far ahead guests may book, 1–365 days) and max concurrent groups (capacity ceiling per slot).
  • Home page — the public tagline.
  • Notifications — the staff inbox that gets “new booking request” alerts (blank turns it off).
  • Support contact and pre-visit details (map link, directions, parking, arrival contact) included in reminder emails.

Each property’s catalog (“Manage catalog →”) has two panels:

  • Services (the disciplines guests can book) — add, edit, reorder, or deactivate. When editing a service you choose which add-ons are offered with it (and can create new add-ons inline).
  • Add-ons (priced extras) — add, edit the price, reorder, or deactivate.

Active items appear in the public funnel; inactive ones don’t. You can’t hard-delete a service or add-on that a booking ever used — deactivate it instead. Catalog changes flow straight through to what guests see.

11. FAQ & Gear templates

This is the reusable content library that auto-fills onto new bids. Manage two tabs — FAQ and Gear — each item scoped to Global, a property, a discipline, or a booking type, so the right items land on the right bids automatically.

Editing an item here only changes future bids — already-sent bids keep the copy they were created with.

12. Waivers

The Waivers page is the signed-waiver record. It has:

  • Walk-in kiosk links — a per-property “Open kiosk” button to launch the iPad sign-in-person page at the property (no login needed).
  • A searchable table of every signed waiver — name, email, source (the booking or walk-in it came from), signed time, and a View PDF link.

Admin only Editing the waiver templates (the wording each property uses) is reserved for admins — see the admin guide. Property managers see their own property’s waivers; admins see all.

13. Common workflows

The everyday jobs, step by step, using the real button names. Start here when you’re learning the portal.

⭐Turn a new request into a confirmed bid

When: a guest just submitted a booking — it’s sitting in Needs review.

  1. Open Bids, click the Needs review pill, and open the new bid.
  2. Read the booking, guest contact, and any guest notes / special requests.
  3. Tap Edit bid content. Add schedule notes, then pull from the library for the gear list and FAQ; adjust as needed and Save changes.
  4. In Pricing, set the Confirmed quote and Deposit, and add a quote note if it differs from the estimate.
  5. Re-read it all (this is exactly what the guest will see), then tap Confirm → Confirm bid.

Result: the guest’s page unlocks signing & paying and they get an email. The bid moves to Active · Awaiting guest.

📞Book for a guest who called in

When: someone phones or emails instead of using the website.

  1. From Bookings, tap Book for a customer.
  2. Walk the funnel as if you were them — property, type, disciplines, guests, and date/time (or instructor, for a lesson).
  3. On the details step, enter the customer’s name, email, and phone (a banner reminds you you’re booking on their behalf).
  4. Submit. It creates their bid, attributed to you — then price & confirm it like any other.

🚫Decline a request you can’t accommodate

When: the date’s full, or it isn’t something the property can host.

  1. Open the bid (it must still be in review).
  2. Tap Deny and type an internal reason — for your records; the guest won’t see it.
  3. Tap Deny bid. The guest is notified the bid is closed.

💸Refund a guest who has to cancel

When: a guest paid a deposit but can no longer come.

  1. Open their (paid) bid and tap Refund deposit.
  2. Confirm the amount (full by default; edit it for a partial) and add a reason.
  3. Tap Refund $X.

Result: Stripe returns the money, the bid moves to refunded, and the booking cancels — the slot frees up automatically.

🔗A bid link got shared too widely

When: you suspect a guest’s private link has leaked.

  1. Open the bid and tap Rotate on the Bid URL card, then confirm.
  2. Copy the new link (shown once) and send it to the guest.

Result: the old link stops working immediately; only the new one opens the bid.

🧭Stand up a new Adventure and fill it

When: the team has lined up a curated member trip.

  1. Adventures → New adventure. Fill in basics, pricing & payment mode, hero/gallery images, and chapters.
  2. Leave it Draft while you polish (only staff can see it via “View public page”); flip to Published when it’s ready.
  3. As members reserve, work the roster: Confirm inquiry leads, Refund & cancel or Cancel as needed, and Export CSV ↓ for the property.

🎯Add a new discipline or add-on to a property

When: a property starts offering a new activity or upsell.

  1. Properties → [property] → Manage catalog.
  2. + Add service, name it, write the guest-facing description, and mark it Active.
  3. In the service editor, tick the add-ons offered with it — or create a new add-on inline.

Result: it appears in the public booking funnel right away.

🖊️Collect waivers at the property

When: a booked group is arriving, or a walk-in shows up.

  • For a booking: open the bid → Scan-to-sign QR; each guest scans and signs on their own phone (tap Next person between them). Or Sign in person on an iPad.
  • For a walk-in (no booking): Waivers → Open [property] kiosk on an iPad, hand it over, then Sign another for the next guest.

15. Good to know

  • Times are Central Time (CT) everywhere — all three properties share one timezone.
  • The bid is the spine. A booking with a bid always routes to the bid detail; that’s where editing happens.
  • Money flows through Stripe. Refunding a bid’s deposit cancels the booking and frees the slot.
  • Waivers are versioned. Editing a template never alters an already-signed waiver.
  • Destructive deletes are guarded. Instructors, services, add-ons, and adventures can’t be hard-deleted once a booking/RSVP references them — the system steers you to deactivate/retire instead.
  • “You don’t have permission” is by design — it means the action is outside your role’s scope, not that something broke.
