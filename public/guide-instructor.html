Instructor Guide · “Gameplan”

Teaching with Rhythm

Your portal is a clean, read-only briefing of the lessons you’re teaching — plus the two things you keep current: your profile and your schedule. Designed for your phone, on the way to meet a guest.

The portal is branded “Rhythm · Gameplan.” Everything you see about lessons is read-only — staff handle the bookings. The only things you set are your profile and your schedule.

2. Getting access & signing in

You never sign yourself up. The flow is:

  1. An admin creates your instructor record and sends your invite in one step.
  2. Click the invite link — it signs you in and links your account. From then on you’re routed straight to /instructor.
  3. Next time, sign in at the usual login (the same page everyone uses) — there’s no separate instructor URL. You’ll land on your gameplan automatically.

Instructors are not staff — you don’t have access to the admin, member, or partner areas, and you don’t need them.

Heads up. If your invite email doesn’t arrive (email delivery is still being finalized), an admin can generate a fresh “Resend link” sign-in URL and hand it to you directly.

3. Setting up your profile

Open Profile (“Your profile”). This is what guests see when they pick an instructor for a lesson, so it’s worth filling in. You can use the portal right away — there’s no hard setup gate — but if your bio or photo is missing you’ll see a gentle “Complete your profile” nudge on your dashboard.

🧑‍🏫

Name

Required — how you’re shown to guests and staff.

📝

Bio

A short guest-facing background — your experience, your style, what a lesson with you is like.

📷

Photo

A square headshot looks best. Upload any size — we resize & compress to web-ready format automatically.

📞

Phone

Your contact number.

Tap Save profile. That’s it — guests booking a private lesson now see your bio and photo in the instructor picker.

4. Setting your schedule

Your schedule is the heart of the job — it decides which times guests can book with you. It’s on the same Profile page, under “Your schedule.” There are two parts.

Weekly hours (your normal pattern)

One box per property you teach at. For each day of the week you’ll see either Unavailable or one or more start–end time windows. Controls:

  • + Add hours under a day to add a window (you can have several per day).
  • Remove next to any window.
  • Fill week with 9–5 — a quick start that drops a 9:00–17:00 window on every weekday that doesn’t already have one. Then trim the days you don’t want.
  • Save weekly schedule — saves the whole grid at once.

Time off & one-off availability (exceptions)

For specific dates that differ from your normal pattern. Use Add an exception:

  • Time off — block your availability on a date (whole day, or a time window; for one property or all of them). Add an optional reason like “Vacation.”
  • Extra hours — open availability outside your normal pattern. These must name a specific property and a specific time window.

Remove any exception with its Remove button.

What a guest actually sees. The booking system combines your weekly hours, your time off and extra hours, the discipline you’re qualified to teach, your existing bookings (with a travel buffer so you’re never double-booked too close together at two properties), and the property’s capacity — and only offers guests the slots that survive all of that.

If you’re not assigned to any property yet, you’ll see “No properties assigned” — ask an admin to add you to a property first.

5. Your gameplan (dashboard)

Your home screen is “Your gameplan” — “Upcoming events you’re teaching. Tap one to see the guest, the activity, and anything they’ve asked for before you meet them.”

It lists your upcoming, confirmed lessons, soonest first. Each event card shows:

  • The date, start time (Central Time), and duration
  • The guest’s name and party size
  • Activity chips — the discipline(s) booked
  • The property

Only confirmed, future lessons appear — there’s nothing to prep for a pending, cancelled, or finished one. If you have none: “No upcoming events. Anything new on your schedule will show up here.”

6. The event briefing

Tap any event to open its full briefing — everything you need before you meet the guests, in scan order. It’s entirely read-only.

SectionWhat’s in it
HeaderDate · start–end time (CT), the primary guest’s name, party size, and property. The guest’s phone and email are tappable — call or email straight from your phone.
ActivityThe discipline chips, plus any schedule notes staff added.
Special requestsAnything the guest typed when booking (shown only if present).
Guests (waivers)A “X of N signed” counter. The primary guest shows “Signed” or “Awaiting signature”; anyone who signed via the scan-to-sign QR is listed too. Outstanding guests can sign on their own phone at arrival.
LogisticsDuration, on-arrival contact, parking, directions, and an “Open map” link — when the property has them filled in.

You only ever see your own assigned events — there’s no way to see another instructor’s lessons.

7. What you can & can’t do

✅

You can

  • Edit your name, bio, photo, and phone
  • Set your weekly hours
  • Add time off and extra one-off hours
  • Read the briefing for every lesson you teach
🚫

An admin handles

  • Which properties you teach at
  • Which disciplines you’re qualified to teach
  • Whether your profile is active / visible
  • Creating, editing, or cancelling bookings

Need a change to any of the admin-handled items? Ask your property manager or an admin.

8. Common situations

The handful of things you’ll actually do, step by step.

🌅Your first day — get set up

When: you’ve just accepted your invite.

  1. Open Profile and fill in your name, bio, photo, and phone; tap Save profile.
  2. Scroll to Your schedule. Tap Fill week with 9–5 for a fast start, then trim the days and times you don’t teach.
  3. Tap Save weekly schedule.

Result: guests can now book lessons with you in the times you set.

🏖️You’re taking time off

When: you’ll be away on specific dates.

  1. On Profile → Your schedule, use Add an exception.
  2. Pick the date, set Type → Time off, leave Whole day checked (or set a window), add a reason if you like.
  3. Tap Add exception. Repeat for each day.

Result: guests won’t be offered those times.

➕You’re running a one-off clinic outside your usual hours

When: you want to open extra availability for a special date.

  1. Use Add an exception and set Type → Extra hours.
  2. Choose the property and the From / To time window (extra hours need both).
  3. Tap Add exception.

📋Before you meet a guest

When: you have a lesson coming up.

  1. On Your gameplan, tap the event to open its briefing.
  2. Read the Activity and any Special requests, and check Logistics (arrival, parking, map).
  3. Glance at the Guests roster to see who’s signed. Tap the guest’s phone or email to reach them if you need to.

✍️A guest hasn’t signed their waiver

When: the briefing shows someone still “Awaiting signature.”

  • Nothing to fix in the app — they sign on their own phone via the scan-to-sign QR at arrival (staff have it). Their status flips to “Signed” once they do.

9. Frequently asked questions

Do guests see my whole schedule?

No — only your open slots, as bookable times. Your hours, time off, and other bookings stay private.

How do I add a discipline I can teach?

That’s an admin/roster decision — ask an admin to add it to your profile. Guests only see you for disciplines you’re qualified for.

I’ll be away next week. What do I do?

Add a Time off exception for those dates — whole-day or a window. Guests won’t be offered those times.

Why isn’t a lesson on my gameplan yet?

Only confirmed, upcoming lessons appear. A request that staff haven’t confirmed yet won’t show.

I lost my sign-in link.

Ask an admin to Resend link — they can generate a fresh one for you.
