Member Guide

Your member portal

For people who belong to one of the clubs. See your memberships and household, manage bookings, reserve curated Adventures, and share trips with your party. Membership is invite-only.

Key terms

Membership
Your belonging at a club, identified by a member number and a tier (level).
Household
Several people sharing one membership (e.g. a couple on the same member number, with different emails).
Adventure
A curated, members-only trip you can browse, reserve, and pay for in the portal.
Bid
The booking page where a trip is signed and paid — its status drives your booking’s status.

2. Signing in

Go to /login (“Members’ Entrance”). Membership is invite-only — staff add you from the roster, and you sign in with the exact email you were invited under. There are three ways in:

MethodHow it works
Magic link (default)Enter your email, leave the password blank, and tap “Email me a sign-in link.” Click the link in your inbox — no password to remember. It stays valid for a few days.
Password (optional)Set a password in your profile once, then sign in directly next time. The magic link still works as a backup.
GoogleTap “Continue with Google.” The Google account’s email must be on the roster.

If your email isn’t on the roster (or an invite expired), you’re signed out with an “invite not found” message — reach your property’s membership coordinator to be added.

3. Your dashboard & memberships

After signing in you land on /member — “Welcome back.” The dashboard shows Your memberships. Each card shows the property, your member number and tier, the status, your role (e.g. primary), and your household — “Also on this membership” lists the other people who share it.

One person can belong to several memberships — across properties, or shared in a household — and they all appear here.

What is a household? An optional feature for memberships that include family members. John and Jane can share one member number with different emails — they’re a “household,” and they see each other’s bookings.

Developer Note. The membership dashboard is intentionally light on personal data. It’s a place to see planned trips, your memberships, and to customize the name on your reservations — not a heavy “profile” full of personal information.

4. Booking a visit & your bookings

How to book a visit

Members book through the same booking flow guests use — there’s no separate “new booking” button in the member portal. Start from Book an experience on the main site. Because you’re signed in, your name and email are pre-filled (you can still change them), so it’s a few taps faster for you.

Pick your property, booking type, disciplines, guests, and time exactly as described in the guest guide’s step-by-step. When you submit, your new booking appears here under My bookings, and you finish by signing your waiver and paying any deposit on its bid page (see Sign & pay).

Booking for your household? Anyone on your membership can book — the trip shows up for everyone, labeled “Booked by …”.

My bookings

The My bookings tab lists every booking made by anyone in your household, across all your memberships — date, time, property, type, status, party size, instructor, totals, and who booked it (“Booked by …” when it was someone else on your membership).

Tap a booking for its detail page (“Your trip”): What to expect (the team’s schedule notes), What you’re booked for (disciplines + add-ons), What to bring (gear list, with “required” flags), Good to know (FAQ), and a Billing breakdown (total, deposit, amount paid).

5. Sharing a trip with your party New

On a finalized booking you made (waiver signed and deposit paid), a “Share this trip” card appears. Add an optional personal note, tap Create share link, and copy the link to send to your group.

Anyone you send it to — even non-members — can open it to see the dates, location, what to bring, and good-to-knows. They won’t see pricing or payment. You can update the note or Revoke link at any time (everyone you shared it with then loses access).

6. Member Adventures

Adventures are curated, members-only trips the team organizes. Browse them on the public /adventures pages; reserving happens inside your member account. Your reserved and waitlisted trips live under the Adventures tab (“Your adventures”).

How reserving works

  1. Browse & open a trip. Each adventure has an immersive page — hero image, “at a glance” (when, duration, host, member price), what’s included, and chapters of detail.
  2. Reserve from the bottom bar. The reserve bar adapts to the trip: Reserve & pay (pay in full now), Reserve · deposit (pay a deposit, balance settles with the concierge), or Request to reserve (no payment now — your concierge follows up).
  3. Pick your party size, then continue to payment. Reserving holds your spot for 30 minutes with a live countdown.
  4. Pay with card or bank (a free trip skips payment). On success: “You’re all set — Payment received.”

If your hold lapses, the spot reopens and you’ll see “Your hold was released” with a Reserve again button (the 30-minute timer starts over).

Sold out? Join the waitlist

If a trip is full, the bar reads “Fully reserved — Join the waitlist.” We email you the moment a spot opens — it’s first-come, so reserve quickly.

Your guest manifest

For a confirmed trip with more than one in your party, expand “Your guests” to name everyone joining you (“2 of 4 named”), then Save guests — so we can prepare for your whole party.

Cancelling

Each adventure card has a Cancel action. The confirmation tells you the outcome based on the cancellation window: outside the window you’re refunded; inside the free-cancellation window your payment is non-refundable. Either way, cancelling frees your spot.

7. Your profile

The Profile tab lets you set your display name — the name the portal uses in the header, on membership cards, and as “booked by” on bookings. It’s an app-only nickname; it doesn’t change your login email or Google name.

You can also set a password here to sign in directly next time instead of waiting for an emailed link. Sign out anytime from the header.

Names already captured on existing bids and bookings stay as they were — changing your display name doesn’t rewrite past trips.

8. Common scenarios

A few things members do most, start to finish.

🔑Signing in for the first time

When: staff just invited you.

  1. Go to /login and enter the email you were invited under.
  2. Leave the password blank and tap “Email me a sign-in link.” Click the link in your inbox.
  3. Optional: open Profile and set a password so you can sign in directly next time.

⛺Grab a spot before an Adventure sells out

When: a trip you want is open and filling up.

  1. Open the adventure and use the bottom bar — Reserve & pay, Reserve · deposit, or Request to reserve.
  2. Pick your party size and continue. Your spot is held for 30 minutes with a countdown.
  3. Pay before the timer runs out. If it lapses, the spot reopens and you can Reserve again.

Result: “You’re all set — Payment received,” and your trip appears under Adventures.

👥Name the guests coming with you

When: you reserved more than one spot on a trip.

  1. Go to Adventures → your trip and expand “Your guests.”
  2. Type a name for each additional seat, then tap Save guests.

📨Invite friends to a trip you booked

When: your booking is finalized (signed and paid) and you want to share the plan.

  1. Open the booking and find “Share this trip.”
  2. Add an optional personal note, tap Create share link, and Copy it to send.
  3. Change your mind later? Revoke link — anyone you shared it with loses access.

Result: friends see the date, location, and what to bring — but never pricing or payment.

↩️Cancel an Adventure

When: you can no longer make a trip.

  1. Go to Adventures → the trip card → Cancel.
  2. Read the refund outcome — it depends on the free-cancellation window — then confirm.

9. Frequently asked questions

How do I get a membership?

By invitation only. Staff add your email to the roster; you sign in with that exact email.

I’m on a household — whose bookings do I see?

Everyone’s on your membership. Bookings made by another household member are labeled “Booked by …”.

Can I reserve an Adventure if I’m not a member at that club?

No — Adventures are reserved for members of the hosting property. You’ll see “Members only.” Reach the concierge about joining.

Do I have to set a password?

No. The emailed magic link works forever; a password is just a shortcut.
