Role & scope

1. Super-admin vs admin

Both super_admin and admin have full access — in practice the two are interchangeable. There’s no control one has that the other lacks; the labels just record seniority. Throughout this guide, “admin” means either.

You hold every capability in the staff guide plus three privileged areas the other staff roles can’t touch: the Team roster, creating/deleting instructors, and waiver templates.

2. First sign-in & the nav

  1. Accept your invite by email, then on the Welcome screen set your Full name and tap “Continue to the portal” (a one-time step).
  2. Your profile (top-right) lets you edit your name and set an optional password.

Your nav carries the full set: Dashboard, Bids (badged with the pending count), Bookings, Instructors, Adventures, Members, Properties, FAQ & Gear, Waivers, Team, and What’s New. The Team link is visible only to admins.

3. The permission model

Two gates protect every action — understanding them helps you reason about what each teammate can do.

  1. The portal gate. Each role is allowed into exactly one portal. The five staff roles land in /admin; members in /member; instructors in /instructor. A role can’t cross into another portal.
  2. The data gate. Inside /admin, the database itself narrows what each role can read and write. That’s why a concierge sees the same screens as you but gets “You don’t have permission…” on actions outside their lane — the data, not the page, is what’s protected.

4. The shared day-to-day (quick tour)

This is the same work every staff role does — summarized here so this guide stands alone. (The staff guide covers it in full depth.)

Dashboard

A read-only ops snapshot: Pending review, Recent activity, Confirmed · next 24 hours, and Upcoming · next 7 days by property. Every row links into detail. Times are Central (CT).

Working a bid

The core job. From Bids, filter (Needs review / Active / Closed, plus search, property, dates) and open a bid. Then:

  • Edit bid content — schedule notes, gear list, FAQ (markdown), and internal staff notes; pull from the FAQ & Gear library.
  • Pricing — set the confirmed quote, deposit, and a quote note; “Apply suggested quote” when add-ons changed.
  • Confirm (unlocks signing & paying and emails the guest) or Deny (with an internal reason).
  • Copy / Rotate the secure link, Refund a paid deposit (refunding cancels the booking and frees the slot), and collect waivers (scan-to-sign QR, in-person iPad, view PDFs).

Verify price and content before confirming — confirming reveals everything to the guest.

A bid moves through a fixed set of statuses — and the guest sees a friendlier label on their own page:

Bookings · Members · Adventures · Properties · Templates · Waivers

  • Bookings — a calendar by property; “Book for a customer” runs the funnel on someone’s behalf. Bookings with a bid route to the bid.
  • Members — a read-only directory of memberships, households, bookings, and adventure RSVPs (no impersonation).
  • Adventures — create/edit curated trips (basics, pricing & payment mode, visibility, imagery, chapters); manage the roster (confirm / refund & cancel / cancel) and export CSV.
  • Properties & catalog — booking horizon, capacity, tagline, notification email, pre-visit details; services & add-ons (reorder, link, deactivate). Active items show in the public funnel.
  • FAQ & Gear — the content library that auto-fills bids; edits affect future bids only.
  • Waivers — the signed-waiver record, kiosk launch links, and PDF views.

The shared day-to-day, plus three privileged areas only admins can touch

5.1 Team management

The Team nav item (admins only) is where you add and manage staff.

  • Invite a teammate — enter their email, pick a role (the five staff roles), and a property (for property managers). They set their own name on first sign-in.
  • Edit role — change someone’s role (and property) later.
  • Deactivate / Reactivate — a reversible soft-revoke that clears their access but keeps the record.
  • Resend link — generate a fresh sign-in link for someone who hasn’t signed in yet.
  • Remove — permanently delete the account (immediate cut-off).

You can’t change, deactivate, or remove your own row. And note: someone already signed in keeps access until their session refreshes — deactivation isn’t always instant for an active session.

5.2 Creating & deleting instructors

Viewing and scheduling instructors is open to all staff (and the property manager for their own property), but creating and deleting instructors are admin-only.

  • Add an instructor — enter their name, email (required), optional phone, and the properties they’re “Available at.” One click creates the record and sends their portal invite. (If the email’s already in use, the record is still created — just retry the invite.)
  • From there, build their profile (photo, bio, disciplines, active/visibility, order) and their schedule — see the instructor guide for what they’ll experience.
  • Delete instructor — removes the profile and login, but is blocked if they have any bookings. To retire someone with history, toggle them inactive instead — they vanish from the public picker and auto-assignment while their past lessons stay intact.

5.3 Waiver templates

The wording guests agree to. Under Waivers → Edit waiver templates, there’s one editor card per property with a Title, Body, and Consent text (all required).

Saving creates a new immutable version and activates it. Every previously signed waiver keeps the exact version its guest agreed to — frozen into that signed PDF. You’re never rewriting history, only changing what future signers see.

Admins see all properties’ signed waivers; property managers see only their own.

Developer Note. Waivers are signed with our own in-house e-signature, not a paid vendor — a rendered PDF plus a tamper-evidence hash and audit trail (typed name, time, IP). The old Dropbox Sign path is retained but deprecated behind a single switch, for if a future need (multi-party signing, identity proofing) ever arises.

6. Common workflows

Step-by-step recipes for the things admins do most. Each one uses the real button names so you can follow along on screen.

🧑‍🏫When a new instructor joins

When: you’re bringing a new lesson instructor onto the platform.

  1. Go to Instructors → Add an instructor. Enter their name, email (required), optional phone, and tick every property under “Available at.” One click creates their record and emails their portal invite.
  2. Open their profile with Edit profile →. Add a photo and bio (guests see these in the lesson picker), then tick the disciplines they’re qualified to teach at each property.
  3. Set Active on, and a display order if you want them to sort first.
  4. Decide who sets the hours: the instructor can set their own weekly hours & time off when they sign in, or you can do it now via Weekly schedule & time off →.
  5. Make sure they actually got in. If the invite email didn’t arrive, use Resend link on their roster row and send them that sign-in URL directly.

Result: the instructor now appears in the private-lesson picker for their disciplines, guests can book times that fit their schedule, and the instructor sees their lessons in the Gameplan portal.

Tip: created the wrong record after they already have a booking? Don’t delete it — toggle Active off instead, which preserves history.

👋Onboard a new staff member

When: a new teammate needs access to the admin portal.

  1. Team → Add a team member. Enter their email, pick a role, and (for a property manager) their property.
  2. Tap Send invite. They click the link, set their own Full name on the Welcome screen, and they’re in.

Tip: pick the narrowest role that fits — concierge or membership_coordinator for day-to-day, property_manager to scope someone to one club, and admin only for full trust.

🔁Change someone’s role or property

When: a teammate’s responsibilities change.

  1. Team → find their row → Edit role.
  2. Choose the new role (and property), then Save.

Note: if they’re currently signed in, the change takes effect when their session refreshes — not always instantly.

🚪Someone leaves the team

When: offboarding a staff member.

  • Reversible: Deactivate their row — clears access but keeps the record; Reactivate later.
  • Permanent: Remove — deletes the account and cuts access immediately.

Note: you can’t deactivate or remove your own row.

🎯An instructor stops teaching

When: an instructor is leaving or pausing.

  • Keep their history: open their profile and toggle Active off — they drop out of the picker and auto-assignment, past lessons intact.
  • Cut portal access only: Revoke on their row removes the login but keeps the record for an easy re-invite.
  • Delete only works if they never had a booking.

📄Update a property’s waiver wording

When: legal hands you new waiver language.

  1. Waivers → Edit waiver templates → the property’s card.
  2. Edit the Title, Body, and Consent text, then Save.

Result: a new version activates for future signers. Everyone who already signed keeps the exact version they agreed to — nothing is rewritten.

7. Good to know

  • Times are Central Time (CT) across all three properties.
  • The bid is the spine. Bookings with a bid route to the bid detail — that’s where curation and pricing happen.
  • Refunds cancel. Refunding a deposit moves the bid to refunded and releases the slot.
  • Versioned waivers & future-only templates mean edits never alter what someone already signed or was already quoted.
  • Destructive deletes are guarded — once a booking, RSVP, or quote references something, the system makes you deactivate rather than delete, preserving history.
  • Operational knobs live in the app, not in code — horizons, capacities, taglines, contacts, payment modes are all editable by admins on the relevant screens.
