Booking a visit

Everything a guest does on the public website — choose an experience, get a confirmed quote, sign your waiver, and pay your deposit. No account required.

Key terms

Bid
Your personal booking page — it holds your confirmed price, schedule, waiver, and deposit. You sign and pay here.
Booking type
Plan a Visit, Private Lesson, or Host an Occasion.
Deposit
Paid up front to lock your date; the balance settles at the property.
Add-on
An optional priced extra — ammunition, premium gear, an instructor upgrade.
Waiver
The liability form you sign electronically before your visit.

2. Building your booking, step by step

From the homepage, tap Book an experience. The whole funnel takes about five minutes, with a running summary on the side so you always see what you’ve chosen and an estimated total.

  1. Pick a property. “Where shall we see you?” Choose one of the three clubs. Each card shows its name, locale, and a short tagline.
  2. Choose a booking type.
    TypeBest forLength
    Plan a VisitOpen range time, casual — pick as many disciplines as you likeTwo hours
    Private LessonOne-on-one with an instructor you chooseOne to three hours
    Host an OccasionExclusive use — tournaments, milestones, corporate daysTwo to six hours

    Host an Occasion blocks all other guests at the property during your window, so the team tailors and quotes it after your inquiry.

  3. Build your booking. A little three-part wizard — Disciplines → Guests → When:
    • Disciplines & add-ons. Pick your activities (several for a Visit; exactly one for a Lesson), then layer on optional add-ons — extra ammunition, premium gear, instructor upgrades — each with a price and a quantity stepper.
    • Guests. Slide to set your party size; pricing tiers and per-person fees adjust automatically.
    • When. Pick a date (only dates inside the property’s booking window appear) and a time. Already-reserved times are greyed out and labeled “reserved,” and availability is checked live as you look.
  4. Private Lesson? Choose your instructor first. New For a lesson, the “When” step becomes “Choose your instructor & time.” You’ll see a card for each instructor who teaches your discipline — with their photo, name, and bio — marked Available or No openings in this window. We pre-select the first available instructor; tap a different one to switch. The calendar and time slots then show only that instructor’s free times. Switching instructors clears your date/time, since availability differs per person.
  5. Enter your details. Name and email are required; phone and any notes (“special requests, accessibility needs, who else is coming…”) are optional. Tap Submit booking →.

If you refresh mid-funnel, your progress resets and you’ll see a friendly “Let’s start over” — just pick up from the property step again. It only takes a few minutes.

3. What happens after you submit

The moment you submit, the system creates your booking and a bid (your private booking page) in one step, then emails you a confirmation. You land on your personal bid page at a private link (a unique web address + access code — no login, but keep the link safe).

At first the bid shows “In review.” Staff confirm the details and final price — typically within 24 hours — and you’ll get an email when it’s ready to sign and pay.

Why the wait? Pricing for some experiences is confirmed by a person, not a formula. This is the step where staff lock in your quote, schedule, gear list, and waiver.

Developer Note. Most or all of this could be automated if we build a pricing formula. Nothing stops it — it just has to be planned carefully.

4. Your bid page — sign & pay

Your bid page is the home base for your booking. Once staff confirm it, the full page unlocks: a status badge up top, a small progress timeline, your itinerary, and the two things you need to do — sign and pay.

Your itinerary

A confirmed bid shows who it’s For (your name and party size), what you’re booked for (disciplines and add-ons), What we’ll bring (a gear list), the Schedule (arrive / wrap / duration / your instructor), Things to know (an expandable FAQ), and Getting there.

Sign your waiver

Tap Sign your waiver → to open the waiver. Scroll through the full text (or tap “Skip to bottom”), type your full legal name, check the consent box, and press Confirm & sign. It’s instant — the section flips to “Signed ✓.”

Pay your deposit

Pay by card or bank transfer, right on the page. If the team set a full quote above the deposit, you can choose any amount between the deposit and the full quote — there are quick Deposit and Full buttons. The remainder settles at the property.

The amount locks once you start entering card details (“refresh the page to change it”). Signing and paying are independent — do them in either order. If no deposit is required, signing alone finalizes your booking.

You're all set

When everything required is done, the page celebrates — “You’re all set — we can’t wait to see you.” — and locks in your date. Save this page; it’s your confirmation and everything you need is on it.

Developer Note. The waiver uses our own in-house e-signature — not a third-party service like DocuSign or Dropbox Sign. A single-signer liability waiver isn’t complex enough to justify a paid vendor. We render the signed PDF ourselves, store it privately, and keep a tamper-evidence hash plus an audit trail (typed name, timestamp, IP), which meets the ESIGN/UETA bar for a waiver. The old Dropbox Sign integration is retained but deprecated — flip one setting to switch it back on if a future need (multi-party signing, identity proofing) ever calls for it.

6. Signing a waiver

There’s more than one place you might sign, but it’s always the same idea: read the property’s waiver, type your full legal name, check the consent box, and sign.

WhereWhen you’d use it
On your bid pageThe main way — the “Sign your waiver” step described above.
Scan-to-sign QRAt the property, each person in your party can scan a QR code and sign on their own phone. After you sign, tap Next person and hand the phone along.
Walk-in iPadA staff member may hand you an iPad at the property to sign before you go out — no booking or login needed.

7. Member Adventures (a peek)

You can browse Member Adventures — curated, members-only trips — from the public site at /adventures. Anyone can read the beautiful trip pages, but reserving a spot requires a member account at the hosting club. If you’re not a member yet, you’ll see “Sign in to reserve”; reach out about membership and you’ll get the full experience described in the member guide.

8. Trip links you might be sent

If a member invites you on a trip they booked, you may receive a trip link (a web address starting with /trip/). It opens a clean, read-only overview — the date, location, what to expect, what to bring, and good-to-knows. It deliberately shows no pricing, payment, or contact details. You don’t need an account to view it.

9. Common guest scenarios

Whatever you’re planning, here’s the quickest path through.

🎯A casual afternoon on the range

When: you just want open range time.

  1. Tap Book an experience and pick your property.
  2. Choose Plan a Visit.
  3. Pick your disciplines (as many as you like) and any add-ons, set your guest count, then a date & time.
  4. Enter your details and tap Submit booking →.

🧑‍🏫A private lesson with the instructor you want

When: you want one-on-one coaching.

  1. Book your property and choose Private Lesson.
  2. Pick the one discipline you’d like to learn.
  3. On Choose your instructor & time, pick the instructor you want — then a time that fits their schedule.
  4. Enter your details and submit.

Tip: if an instructor shows “No openings in this window,” try another — availability differs per person.

🎉A corporate day or celebration

When: you want the property to yourselves.

  1. Book your property and choose Host an Occasion.
  2. Set your group size and submit your details — no instant price.
  3. The team builds a custom quote within 24 hours and sends your bid to sign & pay.

✅Finish your booking once it’s confirmed

When: you got the email that your bid is ready.

  1. Open your bid link and tap Sign your waiver → — scroll, type your name, check consent, Confirm & sign.
  2. Tap into the deposit section and pay (choose any amount from the deposit up to the full quote).
  3. Save the page — it’s your confirmation.

🖊️Get your whole group signed at arrival

When: several of you are coming and everyone needs a waiver.

  • At the property, scan the QR code and sign on your phone, then tap Next person and hand it along — each guest signs their own.

10. Frequently asked questions

Do I need an account to book?

No. Booking, signing, and paying all happen on your private bid link without logging in.

Why didn’t a price show up instantly?

Many experiences are priced by staff. You get an estimate while booking; the confirmed quote arrives when staff confirm your bid (usually within 24 hours).

Can I choose my instructor?

Yes — for a Private Lesson. You pick the instructor first, and the calendar then shows only the times that instructor is free.

I lost my bid link. What now?

Check your confirmation email, or contact the team — staff can re-send or re-issue your secure link.

How do I become a member?

Membership is invite-only. Contact the club and staff will add you to the roster.
