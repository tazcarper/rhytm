iWhat Rhythm Outdoors Is

Rhythm Outdoors is a single web application that runs three outdoor sporting clubs from one place:

  • Horseshoe Bay Sporting Club
  • Hog Heaven Sporting Club
  • Packsaddle Precision

One website, one backend, one database — but several different “front doors,” called portals, each built for a different kind of person.

PortalWho it's forWhat they do there
PublicAnyone on the internet — prospective guestsBrowse, build a booking, sign a waiver, and pay a deposit
MemberLogged-in club membersSee memberships, household, and upcoming trips; reserve Adventures; edit their profile
Instructor GameplanLesson instructorsSet their teaching schedule and read a briefing for each lesson they teach
AdminStaff (five roles)Review and price bids, manage bookings, configure properties, instructors, and pricing

The one idea behind everything

Every inquiry should end as a signed bid page with a deposit already paid — no phone tag, no five tabs open, no spreadsheets. A guest tells us what they want, staff confirms the details and price, and the guest signs and pays on a single web page that becomes their confirmation.

Roles at a glance

Your role decides which portal you land in. The platform checks it on every page.

RolePortalIn short
super_admin/adminFull staff access, including the team roster, instructors, and waiver templates.
admin/adminSame powers as super-admin — the two are interchangeable in practice.
property_manager/adminFull day-to-day, scoped to their own property.
concierge/adminDay-to-day bid, booking, and guest work.
membership_coordinator/adminDay-to-day work with a membership focus.
member/memberA club member — invite-only.
instructor/instructorTeaches lessons; sees a read-only “gameplan.”
partner—Reserved for future outside partners; no portal is built yet.

Portals don’t overlap. A member can’t see the admin tools; an admin doesn’t sign in as a member; an instructor isn’t staff. Each portal is a clean, single-purpose surface — which is exactly why there’s a separate guide for each.

⚙The technology behind it

Rhythm Outdoors is one web application, not a patchwork of separate systems. It runs on Next.js and leans on a handful of specialist services for the things a website shouldn't try to build itself — taking payments, sending email. The golden rule: our own database is the single source of truth. Everything else either feeds into it or reads from it.

One app at the center, specialist services around the edge — the database is the source of truth
ToolJobWhat it actually does for us
Next.jsThe applicationBuilds every page and handles every request — the public funnel, the member, instructor, and admin portals are all one Next.js app.
VercelHostingRuns the app on the internet, deploys new versions, and serves preview links for work in progress.
SupabaseDatabase & authThe Postgres database that stores bookings, bids, members, pricing, and properties — plus the sign-in system. This is the source of truth.
StripePaymentsCollects deposits and adventure payments (card or bank transfer) and processes refunds when staff issue them.
ResendEmailSends the automated emails — booking confirmations, “your bid is ready to sign,” receipts, reminders.
Homegrown waiverE-signaturesPowers the “sign your waiver” step in-house — a typed signature, a rendered PDF, and a tamper-evidence audit trail. (A Dropbox Sign path remains in the code, deprecated, behind a switch.)
Vercel BlobFile storageHolds uploaded images and the signed waiver PDFs the app keeps privately.
InngestBackground workflowsRuns multi-step jobs behind the scenes — reminder emails, adventure follow-ups — without making anyone wait.
HubSpotCRM (downstream)Receives customer and booking information for sales and marketing. It reads from us; it is never the source of truth.
Sentry + AxiomMonitoring (planned)Will track errors and logs so the team can spot and diagnose problems quickly. Planned for closer to launch.

Why so many services? Each one is best-in-class at a single hard job — taking a payment safely, delivering email reliably. We connect them rather than rebuild them, and keep all the decisions and records in our own database.
