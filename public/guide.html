What Rhythm Outdoors Is

Rhythm Outdoors is a single web application that runs three outdoor sporting clubs from one place:

  • Horseshoe Bay Sporting Club
  • Hog Heaven Sporting Club
  • Packsaddle Precision

One website, one backend, one database — but three different “front doors,” called portals, each built for a different kind of person.

PortalWho it's forWhat they do there
PublicAnyone on the internet — prospective guestsBrowse, build a booking, sign a waiver, and pay a deposit
MemberLogged-in club membersSee memberships, household, and upcoming trips; edit their profile
AdminStaff (five roles)Review and price bids, manage bookings, configure properties and pricing

The one idea behind everything

Every inquiry should end as a signed bid page with a deposit already paid — no phone tag, no five tabs open, no spreadsheets. A guest tells us what they want, staff confirms the details and price, and the guest signs and pays on a single web page that becomes their confirmation.

Words you'll see throughout this guide

TermWhat it means
BookingA request to visit a property on a date — who's coming, what they'll do, for how long.
BidThe page a guest receives for a booking. Holds the confirmed price, schedule, waiver, and deposit. The bid is what a guest signs and pays.
Discipline / ServiceAn activity offered at a property (e.g. a shooting discipline, a lesson type).
Add-onAn optional priced extra attached to a service (e.g. an ammo package, a guide rental).
Booking typeOne of three formats: Plan a Visit, Private Lesson, or Host an Occasion.
DepositThe amount paid up front on the bid page to lock the date. The balance settles at the property.
WaiverThe liability document the guest signs electronically (via Dropbox Sign) before the visit.
PropertyOne of the three clubs. Each has its own services, prices, capacity, and booking window.

1.2 Step-by-step: from landing page to confirmed trip

Step 1 — Land. The homepage explains the five-step promise. Tap Book an experience.

Step 2 — Choose a property. Pick one of the three clubs. Each card shows its name, location, and a short tagline.

Step 3 — Choose a booking type:

TypeBest forLength
Plan a VisitOpen range time, casual~2 hours
Private LessonOne-on-one with an instructor1–3 hours
Host an OccasionExclusive use, tournaments, corporate days2–6 hours

Step 4 — Build the booking. On one screen you set the number of guests, pick a date (only dates within the property's booking window appear), pick a time slot (full slots are greyed out — availability is checked live), and choose your disciplines and any add-ons. A running summary shows an estimated total as you go.

Step 5 — Enter your details. Name and email are required; phone and notes are optional. Tap Submit Your Booking.

1.3 What happens after you submit

The moment you submit, the system creates your booking and a bid in one atomic step, then emails you a confirmation. You land on your personal bid page at a private link (a unique slug + access code — no login, but keep the link safe).

At first the bid shows “In review.” Staff confirm the details and final price — typically within 24 hours — and you'll get an email when it's ready to sign and pay.

Why the wait? Pricing for some experiences is confirmed by a person, not a formula. This is the step where staff lock in your quote, schedule, gear list, and waiver.

Developer Note. Most / All of this COULD be automated if we can build a formula for pricing. There is nothing stopping the development of this, but it must be planned carefully.

1.4 Signing and paying on the bid page

Once staff confirm your bid, returning to your bid link unlocks the full page: your schedule, the gear list, FAQ and contact info, a Sign your waiver section (Dropbox Sign, in-page), and a Pay your deposit section if required (Stripe — card or bank transfer).

Both signing and paying are typically required to finalize, and you can do them in either order. If no deposit is required, signing alone finalizes the booking. When all requirements are met, the page celebrates — “You're all set” — and locks in your date. Save this page; it's your confirmation and everything you need is on it.

1.5 Bid states you might see

StateWhat it means for you
In reviewSubmitted; staff are preparing your quote. Nothing to do yet.
ConfirmedReady to sign and pay.
Signed / Paid / BookedYou've completed your part — you're set.
ExpiredThe bid sat unsigned/unpaid too long. Contact the team for a fresh one.
DeniedThe booking couldn't be accommodated. Contact the team.
RefundedA paid deposit was returned (e.g. a cancellation).

2.2 Logging in

Go to /login (“Members' Entrance”) and choose Magic link (enter your email; we send a one-tap sign-in link, no password) or Google. Either way, only invited emails work. If your email isn't on the roster (or the invite expired), you're signed out with an “invite not found” message — reach out to staff to be added.

2.3 Your dashboard and memberships

After signing in you land on /member. The dashboard shows Your memberships. Each card shows the property, your member number and tier, the status, your role (e.g. primary), and the household — other people who share the membership. One person can belong to several memberships (across properties or shared in a household), and they all appear here.

Developer Note. This is a work in progress. I dont recommend we lean TOO heavy into a "profile" with a ton of personal information. The membership dashboard really is a place to see planned trips, memberships and customize your name for your reservations.

2.4 Your bookings

The My bookings tab lists every booking made by anyone in your household, across all your memberships — date/time, property, type, status, party size, and who booked it. Tap a booking for its detail page: full guest info, schedule, gear list, FAQ, disciplines, add-ons, and the complete pricing breakdown (quote, deposit, amount paid, balance).

What is Household. "Household" an optional feature built that assumes a "membership" could include family members, such as wives. John and Jane could be on the same membership number and use different emails. They would be in a "household".

2.5 Editing your profile

The Profile tab lets you set your display name — the name the portal uses in the header, on membership cards, and as “booked by” on bookings. It's an app-only nickname; it doesn't change your login email or Google name. (Names already captured on existing bids and bookings stay as they were.) Sign out anytime from the header.

3.2 Who can sign in

Five staff roles have access to /admin: super_admin, admin, property_manager, concierge, membership_coordinator. Admins intentionally do not have access to the member or partner portals — staff see member data through admin views, not by logging in as a member.

3.3 The dashboard

The first screen is an operations snapshot: a pending review count and the most recent waiting bids; confirmed bookings · next 24 hours grouped by property (today and tomorrow); and upcoming · next 7 days, one column per property. Everything links into a detail page; the dashboard itself is read-only.

3.4 Working a bid: review, price, confirm, send

This is the core of the admin job. From the Bids queue (filter by status, property, date range, or search by guest), open a bid to see the full picture: booking details, guest contact, disciplines and add-ons, the pricing breakdown, lifecycle timestamps, and internal staff notes.

Tap Edit and set

  • Confirmed quote — the price the guest sees (overrides the auto-estimate)
  • Deposit — the minimum due before the trip
  • Schedule notes, a gear list, and FAQ entries
  • Staff notes (internal only)

Then act on the bid

ActionWhen it's availableWhat it does
ConfirmBid is in reviewUnlocks the schedule, waiver, and deposit on the guest's bid page; emails the guest.
DenyBid is in reviewMarks the bid denied with an internal reason; guest is notified (they don't see the reason).
Regenerate URLActive bidsIssues a new secure link and invalidates the old one (shown once — copy it).
Refund depositDeposit was paidReturns the deposit to the guest's card via Stripe.

Always verify the price and bid content before confirming — confirming is what reveals it all to the guest.

3.5 Bookings

The Bookings list mirrors the bids list (same filters and columns) and is largely a read-only record. Most bookings have an associated bid; opening such a booking takes you straight to its bid, which is where the editing happens.

3.6 Property settings

/admin/properties shows one editable card per property. Staff can set the booking horizon (how far ahead guests may book, 1–365 days), max concurrent groups (capacity ceiling per slot), the tagline on the public homepage, and the support email / phone shown to guests. Changes apply immediately to the public booking funnel.

3.7 The catalog: services and add-ons

Each property's catalog (/admin/properties/[id]/catalog) has two panels. Services (the disciplines guests can book) can be added, edited, reordered, or deactivated — deactivating warns about affected bookings first. Add-ons (priced extras) can be added, edited (including price), reordered, or deactivated. When editing a service, choose which add-ons are offered with it — you can even create new add-ons inline. Catalog changes flow straight through to what guests see in the booking funnel.

4.3 The technology behind it

Rhythm Outdoors is one web application, not a patchwork of separate systems. It runs on Next.js and leans on a handful of specialist services for the things a website shouldn't try to build itself — taking payments, sending email, collecting signatures. The golden rule: our own database is the single source of truth. Everything else either feeds into it or reads from it.

What each piece does

ToolJobWhat it actually does for us
Next.jsThe applicationBuilds every page and handles every request — the public booking funnel, the member portal, and the admin tools are all one Next.js app.
VercelHostingRuns the app on the internet, deploys new versions, and serves preview links for work in progress.
SupabaseDatabase & authThe Postgres database that stores bookings, bids, members, pricing, and properties — plus the sign-in system for members and staff. This is the source of truth.
StripePaymentsCollects the deposit on the bid page (card or bank transfer) and processes refunds when staff issue them.
ResendEmailSends the automated emails — booking confirmations, “your bid is ready to sign,” and similar notices.
Dropbox SignE-signaturesPowers the “sign your waiver” step on the bid page, capturing a legal signature in-page.
Vercel BlobFile storageHolds uploaded files and documents the app needs to keep.
InngestBackground workflowsRuns the multi-step jobs that happen behind the scenes after an action — e.g. follow-up steps once a bid is confirmed — without making the guest wait.
HubSpotCRM (downstream)Receives customer and booking information from the app for sales and marketing. It reads from us; it is never the source of truth.
Sentry + AxiomMonitoring (planned)Will track errors and logs so the team can spot and diagnose problems quickly. Planned for closer to launch.

Why so many services? Each one is best-in-class at a single hard job — taking a payment safely, delivering email reliably, capturing a legal signature. We connect them rather than rebuild them, and keep all the decisions and records in our own database.

4.4 Frequently asked questions

Does a guest need an account to book?

No. Booking, signing, and paying all happen on a private bid link without logging in.

Why didn't a price show up instantly?

Many experiences are priced by staff. The guest gets an estimate during booking; the confirmed quote arrives when staff confirm the bid (usually within 24 hours).

How do members get access?

By invitation only. Staff add the member's email to the roster; the member signs in with that exact email via magic link or Google.

Can an admin log in as a member?

No. Staff view member data through admin screens, not by entering the member portal. This keeps each portal single-purpose and easy to secure.

Where do pricing and availability actually live?

In the database, which all three portals read from. HubSpot and other tools are downstream of it, never the source of truth.
