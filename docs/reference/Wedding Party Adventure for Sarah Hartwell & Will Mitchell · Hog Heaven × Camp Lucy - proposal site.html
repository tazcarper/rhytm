Recommended attire

Dress for Hill Country.

Outdoor range, active afternoon, possible sun. Pass this list along to the wedding party so nobody arrives in flip-flops.

Long Pants

Jeans, chinos, or technical pants. No shorts on the range — protection from sun, brush, and ejected casings.

Closed-Toe Shoes

Boots, sneakers, or trail shoes. No sandals or flip-flops. Boots strongly preferred for the bridge crossing.

Long-Sleeve Shirt

Breathable fabric recommended. No tank tops or open shoulders. Casings get warm.

Ball Cap or Hat

Strongly recommended. The brim catches casings and shades the eyes from afternoon glare.

Layers

June afternoons in the Hill Country can swing 15° between 3 PM and sunset. A light vest is rarely a mistake.

Sunglasses

Wraparound or shooting-style preferred over fashion frames — they double as eye protection between stations.

What Not to Wear

White or light-colored clothing (clay dust). Heels. Open-back tops. Anything you'd be heartbroken about staining.

Jewelry & Watches

Removable, please. Recoil and rings don't get along. We'll hold them at the clubhouse if needed.
