The Industry

Outdoor experiences are not hard. Booking them is.

Walk into any sporting club, lodge, or guide service in this country and you'll find the same pattern — a beautiful product surrounded by brittle, manual operations. The customer wants to give you money. The system makes them work for it.

The friction the industry accepts

  • You can rarely book online. You call a number, leave a message, wait for a callback.
  • Pricing isn't published. Every quote is a back-and-forth thread.
  • Waivers feel like a bolt-on — janky third-party forms that break the brand.
  • No one knows availability in real time. Double-bookings are common.
  • Members get the same sluggish experience as walk-ins. Their status doesn't follow them.
  • Hotel and resort partners renegotiate group rates over email, every single time.

Our answer

  • Reactive forms that price the experience in real time as the guest configures it.
  • Member portals that authenticate against status and surface preferred pricing automatically.
  • Partner portals that protect group rates behind a password.
  • Real-time inventory — instructors, ranges, hours — no double-bookings, ever.
  • Bid pages so beautifully crafted guests bookmark them and share them.
  • A waiver flow integrated into the bid itself. No third-party form. No broken brand moment.

The Customer Journey

What a guest experiences, end to end.

Six steps from "I'm curious" to "I'm walking onto the deck." Behind every step, the system has done a dozen things the guest never sees.

1

Inquiry or Application

Guest visits a Rhythm portal — public, member, or partner — and configures what they want: an event, a private lesson, an adventure RSVP, or a membership application. Pricing updates live. Real-time inventory checks instructor and range availability before the form even submits.

2

Acknowledgement

Within seconds, a personalized email from concierge@send.rhythm.co lands in their inbox. The team has the inquiry in HubSpot before the guest has put their phone down.

3

Bid Page

Either auto-generated from the form or quickly assembled by the team, the guest receives a unique, beautifully crafted bid URL — gear list, schedule, map, FAQ, calendar add, share-with-friend, the whole anatomy.

4

Sign + Pay

Digital signature and Square deposit on the same page. Waiver is embedded — no third-party redirect, no broken brand moment. Both events post back to HubSpot. Deal advances to confirmed.

5

The Wait

Automated reminders as the event approaches. If a bid sits unsigned for 24 hours, an automated nudge. Forty-eight hours, a human follows up. The customer is never forgotten and the team never has to remember.

6

The Day

The team arrives knowing exactly who's coming, what they ordered, what they've signed, what they've paid, and what their preferences are. Execution becomes about hospitality, not logistics.

The migration conversations we need to have

Not abstract questions — specific topics with proposed positions. The HubSpot lead's job is to confirm, push back, or refine. The Boundary Document v2.0 is the briefing artifact for the meeting.

Conversation 1 · Schema Mapping

Events & Tournaments DB → HubSpot Deals

Schema is documented and lifecycle-aware. The mapping is straightforward. We need to align with existing Deal pipelines and any custom Deal properties already in the HubSpot build.

Conversation 2 · Member Records

HSB Members → HubSpot Contacts

Membership tier, household relationships, member-specific properties become custom Contact properties. Need to verify no conflicts with existing Contact custom-property design.

Conversation 3 · Adventure Modeling

HSB Member Adventures — Deal or Custom Object?

Multi-day itineraries with party composition may exceed what a Deal can model gracefully. The HubSpot lead's call: type=Adventure on Deals, or a sibling Custom Object?

Conversation 4 · Pricing

Pricing Schema stays in Notion · Products derived

Notion holds the rule layer (the why of every price); HubSpot Products are the runtime materialization (the what on each deal). This preserves Notion's depth without making it a parallel database. Most consequential debate of the meeting.

Conversation 5 · The Boundary

What stays in Notion — permanently

Charter, Properties Master, Operating Agreement, Pay Structures, Policies, Source Documents, FAQ. These are not customer data and don't belong in HubSpot. Confirming this boundary unblocks every downstream decision.

Conversation 6 · Multi-Property

HSB · HH · Packsaddle · Camp Lucy

How does HubSpot model these today? Custom property on Contacts/Deals? Separate Pipelines? A Property custom object? The answer shapes how every report and workflow behaves.

Conversation 7 · Acuity

Stay alongside, or graduate to HubSpot Meetings?

Acuity currently handles instructor scheduling. Phase 7 (real-time inventory) needs an answer. The HubSpot lead's read on HubSpot's calendar primitives is decisive here.

Conversation 8 · Smart Waiver

Retire in Phase 6 in favor of native?

Smart Waiver is a brand-coherence problem — a third-party redirect that breaks the experience. The proposal: replace with a waiver flow embedded directly into bid pages. Same canvas signature, same archival, no redirect. Confirm direction with the HubSpot lead.
